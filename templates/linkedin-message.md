# LinkedIn Message Templates

## Connection Request Note (≤ 300 chars, often ≤ 200 for visibility)

**Best approach**: Mention a genuine shared interest, mutual connection, or specific reason for connecting. Never copy-paste.

**Formula**:
[Specific reason] + [brief credibility] + [what you're hoping for]

**Examples**:
- "Saw your post on [topic] — totally aligned with how I've been thinking about it at [Company]. Would love to connect and exchange ideas."
- "[Mutual name] suggested we connect. I'm exploring [Company] and your background in [X] is exactly the perspective I was hoping to learn from."
- "Fellow [university/bootcamp/community] alum here. I'm exploring opportunities at [Company] and would love to connect."

**Constraints**:
- Max 200-300 characters (keep it short — they see the preview)
- No ask for a job in the connection request itself
- Must feel genuine, not templated

## Follow-up Message (After They Accept)

**Wait**: 1-3 days after connection is accepted before sending.

**Structure**:
1. **Thank them** for connecting (1 brief sentence).
2. **Context/reason** for reaching out — be specific about their role at the company (1-2 sentences).
3. **Your relevant background** — brief, tied to why you're interested in the company (2-3 sentences with a metric).
4. **Soft ask** — a question about their experience at the company, or a request for a short call (1 sentence).

**Constraints**:
- Max 200 words
- Conversational tone — this isn't an email
- Ask about their experience, not for a referral (yet)
- One specific question is better than a vague "can we chat?"

**Good closing questions**:
- "What do you find most exciting about working on [team/product] right now?"
- "I'd love to hear what the [engineering culture / growth / problem space] is like from your POV — open to a quick call?"
- "How has [specific aspect of company] evolved since you joined?"

---

## Direct Outreach to Hiring Manager on LinkedIn

**Use when**: You can't find their email but they're active on LinkedIn.

**Structure**: Same as cold email to hiring manager, but formatted for LinkedIn.

**Constraints**:
- InMail limit: 300 words. Target 150.
- Can use the same subject line approach as email
- Slightly more conversational than email

---

## Anti-Patterns
- "Hi [Name], I hope this message finds you well..."
- "I saw you work at [Company] and I'm interested in opportunities there."
- Asking for a referral in the connection request
- Generic "great content" compliments with no specifics
- Copy-pasting your resume into a LinkedIn message
