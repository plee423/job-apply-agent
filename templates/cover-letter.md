# Cover Letter Template

## When to Use
Cover letters matter most when:
- Applying to a company you deeply care about (show genuine knowledge)
- The role explicitly asks for one
- You're making a non-obvious career move and need to contextualize it
- Applying to a small company where the founder/hiring manager will read it personally

## Structure (3 paragraphs, 350-450 words)

### Opening Paragraph (Hook)
**Never start with**: "I am applying for the position of..."
**Do start with**: A specific, surprising, or compelling observation about the company's work, a problem they're solving, or something you've built/experienced that connects directly to why you want to work there.

**Goal**: Make them want to read the second paragraph. The opening must earn the rest.

**Example opening approaches**:
- "Last year, I spent three months rebuilding our payment reconciliation system from scratch. Halfway through, I realized I was solving the exact problem [Company] built their product to solve — which is part of why I'm writing."
- "When [Company] announced [recent product/milestone/news], I immediately shared it with my team, because we'd been struggling with the exact problem it addresses."
- "[Company]'s approach to [specific thing] is the one I've been arguing for internally for two years."

### Middle Paragraph (Evidence)
Connect your most relevant experience directly to 2-3 of the role's key requirements. This is where you deploy your best STAR story, condensed.

**Structure**:
- What you did (role + scope)
- The specific challenge you solved (that mirrors their need)
- What you did (your unique approach)
- Result (metric)

**Goal**: Make them think "this person has already solved the problem we're trying to hire for."

### Closing Paragraph (Vision + Ask)
**Goal**: Show you understand what winning looks like in this role, and express genuine enthusiasm grounded in specifics.

- 1-2 sentences on what you'd focus on or bring in the first 90 days (shows you've thought about it)
- 1 sentence expressing genuine interest in the conversation
- Confident close — not "I hope to hear from you" but "I'd welcome the chance to discuss how I can contribute"

## Formatting Rules
- No bullet points — prose paragraphs only
- Max 450 words
- Use their company name naturally (not awkwardly)
- Address to a specific person if at all possible
- No "To Whom It May Concern" — research the hiring manager
- Sign with your full name + LinkedIn URL below

## Anti-Patterns
- Starting with "I" as the first word of the letter
- Restating your resume chronologically
- "I have always been passionate about..."
- "I believe I would be a great fit because..."
- Ending with "Thank you for your time and consideration"
- More than 500 words
- Mentioning every job you've ever had
