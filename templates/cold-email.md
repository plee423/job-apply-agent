# Cold Email Template

## Structure

### Version 1: To Hiring Manager / Engineering Leader

**Subject Line** (under 60 chars):
- Formula: [Specific reference to their work/company] + [your unique angle]
- Examples:
  - "Re: your latency post — similar problem at Acme"
  - "Question about [Company]'s [specific product/challenge]"
  - "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"
  - "Built something similar to [their product] — a question"

**Body Structure**:
1. **Opening** (1 sentence): Specific observation about their company, product, or recent news — NOT about the job posting. Shows you did homework.
2. **Bridge** (1-2 sentences): Brief connector from their world to yours. Why you specifically are reaching out to them specifically.
3. **Value** (2-3 sentences): Your most relevant accomplishment, stated concisely with a metric. Tied directly to a problem they likely face.
4. **Ask** (1 sentence): Soft, low-friction ask. "15 minutes" or "happy to share more" — never "an interview."
5. **Sign-off**: First name only is fine. Include LinkedIn URL.

**Constraints**:
- Total body: 100-150 words
- No bullet points — prose only
- One metric minimum
- Subject must be specific to THIS company

---

### Version 2: To Recruiter / Talent Team

**Different angle**: Recruiters care about qualifications matching and availability. Be direct.

**Body Structure**:
1. **Opening** (1 sentence): Brief acknowledgment you're reaching out about the [Role Title] role.
2. **Match** (2-3 sentences): Here are the top 2-3 things from the JD that you match exactly, with evidence.
3. **Differentiator** (1 sentence): One thing that makes you stand out beyond qualifications.
4. **Logistics** (1 sentence): Available, timeline, open to call.
5. **Ask**: Request for a screening call.

**Constraints**:
- Total: 100-130 words
- More direct than hiring manager version
- Mention the role title explicitly

---

## Anti-Patterns to NEVER Use
- "I came across your job posting and..."
- "I am writing to express my interest in..."
- "I would be a great fit because..."
- "Please find my resume attached"
- "I look forward to hearing from you" (too passive)
- "I'm excited/passionate about your company"
- Anything that could work for a different company with a name swap
