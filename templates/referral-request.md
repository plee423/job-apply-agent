# Referral Request Message Template

## Context & Strategy
A referral request is to someone you know (warm connection) asking them to refer you for a role at their company. The goal is to make it as easy as possible for them to say yes.

**Key principles**:
- Respect their time — be brief and specific
- Make the ask easy (a referral, not just "any advice")
- Give them everything they need to say yes (role link, your pitch)
- Don't make them feel obligated — make them feel helpful

---

## For a Warm Connection (Know them personally/professionally)

**Structure**:
1. **Context** (1 sentence): Why you're reaching out to them specifically (they work at the company you're targeting).
2. **The role** (1 sentence): Specific role title + link.
3. **Why you're a fit** (2-3 sentences): Your most relevant experience tied to the role. One metric.
4. **The ask** (1 sentence): Clear ask — a referral or an intro to the hiring team.
5. **Make it easy** (1 sentence): Offer to send your resume/any info they need to refer you.
6. **No pressure close** (1 sentence): Acknowledge that if it's not a good fit or uncomfortable, totally fine.

**Max length**: 150-200 words

**Example structure**:
"Hey [Name], I saw [Company] is hiring for [Role] and immediately thought of you. [Specific role link]. I've spent the last [X years] doing [relevant work] — most recently [brief accomplishment with metric at current company]. This role looks like a perfect match, and I'd love a referral if you're comfortable. Happy to send my resume and a quick summary to make it easy. And if it's not a good time or doesn't feel right, no worries at all — I appreciate you either way."

---

## For a Looser Connection (Met once, LinkedIn connection, same community)

**Adjust**:
- Open by reminding them of the context you met / how you're connected
- Frame it as "would you be open to a quick intro call first" rather than jumping straight to referral
- More humble, less presumptuous tone

**Structure**:
1. Remind them of connection
2. Explain why you're interested in their company
3. Ask for a quick call (15 min) to learn more about the team
4. Only mention the referral as a secondary ask ("if after talking it seems like a fit, I'd love your thoughts on next steps")

---

## Anti-Patterns
- Sending a wall of text asking for multiple things
- Making them feel guilty if they don't help
- Not specifying the role (vague "I'm interested in opportunities at your company")
- Asking for both a call AND a referral AND feedback on your resume in one message
- Being so formal that it feels like a cover letter, not a message to someone you know
