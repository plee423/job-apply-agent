# Slack Message Template

## When to Use
- Reaching out to someone in a shared community Slack (e.g., local tech community, alumni Slack, professional community)
- Messaging an employee in a company's public Slack channel (developer tools companies often have open Slacks)
- Following up after meeting someone briefly at an event

## Key Differences from Email
- Much more casual and conversational
- No subject line
- Shorter — people read Slack differently than email
- Emojis are sometimes appropriate (match the culture)
- Getting a response is the goal, not conveying everything

---

## Structure (for community Slack / open channel)

**Approach**: Lead with genuine engagement, not a job ask. People in community Slacks are protective of their space.

1. **Context** (1 sentence): How you know of them / why you're messaging / shared context.
2. **Genuine observation** (1-2 sentences): Something specific about their work, a post they wrote, a talk they gave, or the company's product — that you genuinely find interesting.
3. **Your brief context** (1 sentence): Who you are, what you work on.
4. **Soft bridge to intent** (1 sentence): Signal interest without making it purely transactional.
5. **Easy ask** (1 sentence): Low-friction — a question, or "open to a quick chat?"

**Max length**: 80-120 words

**Example**:
"Hey [Name] — I've been following your work on [specific thing] for a while and your post about [specific post/topic] really clicked for me. I'm currently a [title] at [company] working on [brief description], and I've been thinking about similar problems. I noticed [Company] is building in this space and I'd love to learn more about what y'all are working on. Any chance you'd be open to a quick chat?"

---

## Structure (for direct Slack DM to an employee)

More like a warm LinkedIn message — slightly more direct since you're specifically reaching out.

1. **Intro** (1 sentence): Who you are and that you're exploring [Company].
2. **Why them** (1 sentence): Specific reason you're reaching out to them vs. anyone else at the company.
3. **Your background** (1-2 sentences): Relevant experience, brief.
4. **Ask** (1 sentence): A question about their experience at the company, or an interest in chatting.

**Max length**: 100 words

---

## Tone Guidance
- Use contractions (you're, I'm, I've)
- A single emoji is fine if the company culture is casual
- Match the formality level to the channel/community culture
- Don't be too salesy — Slack feels more personal than email

## Anti-Patterns
- Cold DM that immediately asks for a job/referral
- Copy-pasting a cold email into Slack
- Long messages with multiple paragraphs
- Sending the same message to multiple people at the same company simultaneously
